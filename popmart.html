Building a Predictor Tool For POPMART's POP NOW Function

Duration: 2.5 months
View Product

What is POP MART & POP NOW?

POP MART is a global lifestyle company that sells collectible figurines through "blind boxes", which are sealed packages containing random items from various series. With a 240% increase in market cap for the past year, POP MART has become a major player in the collectibles industry, tripling its market cap compared to toy maker giants such as Hasbro and Matel. Their digital platform POP NOW allows users to virtually open these blind boxes online, where collectors and fans can browse series, select boxes, and receive hints about what's NOT in each box before making their purchase decision. The excitement comes from not knowing which collectible figure you'll get until you open it, making strategic purchasing decisions crucial for users who want specific rare figures.

Problem?

POP NOW users want to be confident in which blind box to pick.

POP NOW gives out limited hints with a 5 minute window to purchase.
The calculation is too complex to solve manually, and the time limit makes AI chatbots hard to use efficiently.

Currently, users are left to guess and spend more to get their choice item.

Problem Analysis & Discovery

How I Identified the Core Problem

Through systematic analysis of user behavior and pain points, I discovered that the core issue wasn't just about time pressure, but about decision paralysis in a high-stakes, time-constrained environment.

User Research Discovery

Analyzed 100+ user reviews and forum discussions to identify recurring themes: "wasted money," "wrong choices," and "stressful decisions."

Time Constraint Analysis

Mapped the 5-minute decision window against cognitive load requirements, revealing that users need more time than their given window to process hints.

Mathematical Complexity

Discovered that probability calculations for 6+ boxes with multiple exclusions required statistical knowledge most users don't have time for.

Financial Impact

Quantified the cost of poor decisions: users spending 2 more to get desired figures due to suboptimal box selection.

User Testing & Validation

Comprehensive User Validation Strategy

I conducted multi-method user research to validate assumptions and refine the solution through real user feedback.

Community Research

Target: Active POP MART collectors in Discord servers, subreddits, and social media platforms

Method: Observational study

Key Insights:

  • Users shared screenshots of their "failed" attempts
  • Common frustration: "I always pick the wrong box"
  • Desire for "some kind of calculator" was frequently mentioned

Survey Research

Target: 127 POP NOW users via Discord

Key Findings:

  • 78% felt not very confident in their choice of box.
  • 67% felt like the 5-minute purchase window is not enough time to make a decision.
  • Average user attempts 2.3 boxes before getting desired figure

User Interviews

Target: 12 power users (spend $300+ monthly on POP MART)

Method: 15-minute structured interviews

Critical Insights:

  • Users developed personal "systems" but lacked confidence
  • Time pressure caused impulsive purchases leading to overspending and regret.
  • Users misunderstood the probability of getting a specific figure in a box, ending up relying more on intuition.

Design Consistency with POP NOW

The predictor tool deliberately mirrors POP NOW's familiar systems to ensure users feel immediately comfortable and confident.

POP NOW's "NOT ME" System

POP NOW uses the wording "NOT ME" in hints to mark figures not in a box. The same wording is used in the predictor tool for inputting hints.

POP NOW'sBox Status System

POP NOW uses a modal to show NOT ME figures across all boxes in a set. This layout is mimicked for easy comparison and error checking.

Prediction

Quick Customizable Insights

The predictions should provide clear insights based on different user preferences:

  • Users who only want a small selection of products and are indifferent to the rest.
  • Users who are satisfied with most products, but want to avoid a few specific ones.
  • Users who want to maximize the chances of certain products while minimizing the chances of others.

Results are presented in two charts, each chart targeting a different set of user needs.

  • The pie chart shows the chance of getting each figure in the user-selected box. This is useful for users that want a comprehensive view of all possible figures.
  • The bar chart shows the top 5 boxes for the user-selected figure. This is useful for users that want to focus on getting just one specific figure.

Both charts provides a quick overview that enables data-driven decisions.

Two Clear Ways to View Probabilities

Overall Distribution

The pie chart shows probability distribution across all available boxes, giving users a complete overview of their options.

Top Contenders

The bar chart highlights the top 5 boxes for a specific figure, showing exact percentages. This helps users quickly identify the best options and focus their attention on the most promising boxes.

Instant Results

Both visualizations update in real-time as users input hints, providing immediate feedback on how their choices affect probability calculations.
